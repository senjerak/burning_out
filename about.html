Burning Out.

Gabi Lecusay

Who... and What are we?

"Burning Out" is a mordern, interactive sculture that utilizes 'disposable' tech in a recycling initiative to spread more awareness of sustaining Electronic Waste. Utilizing 125 different LED sensor chips found in Electronic Cigarettes, each light represents 20$ to 30$ spent just to contribute to a modern day littering crisis. All together that is 2'500$ worth of electronics that could have been left out in parking lots, parks, and even trash bins.
Sitting on an Arduino powered, 3D-Printed turn table, the Recycled LED cube is seperately controlled with a Raspberry Pi. Connecting the Cube to this website we can see a listed 'Puff' Counter that tracks each and every engagement with the sculpture.


...Why?

The only way to put an end to littering as a whole is to spread awareness on how to properly dispose your electronics and why it is so important to do so to begin with. 125 Electronic Cigarettes is only brushing the tip of the iceberg when it comes to just how many batteries, marketed as disposible products, end up clogging street drains and damaging wildlife from people treating these devices no different than a paper cigarette.


What can You do to help?

By being mindful of your waste and actively separating compost, trash, and recycling you're already one of many who make a huge impact on the world as we know it.
